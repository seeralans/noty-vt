Stochastic Calculus

1. Stochastic Differential Equation

A stochastic differential equation with \(x_t \in \mathbb{R}^d\) has the general form

\begin{equation} \label{} \mathrm{d} x_t = a(x_t, t)\mathrm{d} t + b(x_t, t) \mathrm{d} w_t \end{equation}

2. Ito's Lemma

Let \(x_t \in \mathbb{R}^d\) and \(f: \mathbb{R}^d \to \mathbb{R}\)

\begin{equation} \label{orgad79b49} \label{} \mathrm{d} f(x_t) = \mpdiff{f(x_t)}{x} \cdot \mathrm{d} x_t + \frac{1}{2} x^{\trans}_t \mpdiff[2]{f(x_t)}{x_t} x^{\trans}_t \end{equation}

3. Generator

A generator of a stochastic process is defined

\begin{equation} \label{org66ac51b} (\mathcal{L}f)(x) = \lim_{t \to 0} \frac{1}{t} \lc \mathbb{E}_{x}{\ls f(X_t )\rs} - f(x) \rc, \end{equation}

it describes the infinitesimal evolution of expectations of observables. Suppose one starts with a smooth test function \(f(x)\), the generator describes how this function is deformed by the stochastic process in an infinitesial time. It compares the smooth evolution you would expect from the function \(f\) to the instantaneous changes induced by the underlying stochastic dynamics. The evolution of the expectation of an observable is them

\begin{equation} \label{org01415dc} \diffl{\mathbb{E}[f(X_t)]} = \mathbb{E}[(\mathcal{L}f)(x)] \label{eq-func-evolve} \end{equation}
