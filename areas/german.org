#+title: German Word building


* German Word-Building Cheat Sheet
A practical guide to recognising stems, guessing meanings, and absorbing grammar while reading.

** 1. Prefixes That Change Meaning
*** a) Negating / Reversing
- *un-* → **negates adjectives**
  - glücklich → **unglücklich**
  - klar → **unklar**

- *miss-* → **wrongly / badly**
  - verstehen → **missverstehen**

*** b) Spatial / Directional Prefixes
- *auf-*, *ab-*, *ein-*, *aus-*, *über-*, *unter-*, *vor-*, *nach-*  
  - aufmachen (open)  
  - abnehmen (take off / lose weight)  
  - einsteigen (get in)

*** c) Separability Tip
- *Stressed prefix* → **separable** (auf\|stehen)  
- *Unstressed prefix* → **inseparable** (verstehen)

** 2. Suffixes That Form Adjectives (Often from Nouns)
*** a) Origin / Belonging
- *-isch* → “**-ish / relating to**”
  - Kind → **kindisch**
  - Deutschland → **deutsch**

*** b) Possession / Quality
- *-ig* → “**-y / having**”
  - Salz → **salzig**
  - Mut → **mutig**

- *-lich* → “**-ly / characteristic of**”
  - Freude → **freudig**

- *-reich* → “**rich in**”
  - Nahrung → **nahrreich**

*** c) Resulting From / Tending Toward
- *-bar* → “**-able**”
  - essen → **essbar**

** 3. Suffixes That Form Nouns
*** a) Abstract Nouns
- *-heit / -keit* → “**-ness / -ity**”
  - sicher → **Sicherheit**
  - möglich → **Möglichkeit**
  - freundlich → **Freundlichkeit**

*** b) Turning Verbs into Nouns
- *-ung* → “**-ing / result of**”
  - bewegen → **Bewegung**
  - lernen → (Lernung — rare, but pattern matters)

*** c) People Who Do Things
- *-er / -erin*
  - arbeiten → **Arbeiter / Arbeiterin**
  - lernen → **Lerner / Lernerin**

** 4. Verb-Building Patterns
*** a) Verb + Prefix = New Meaning
- gehen:
  - **ausgehen** (go out)
  - **eingehen** (enter; shrink)
  - **vergehen** (pass, as in time)

*** b) Verbs From Adjectives
- *-en* ending  
  - sauber → **säubern** (to clean)

*** c) Verbs From Nouns
- Common prefixes: *be-*, *ver-*, *ent-*
  - **befreunden** (to befriend)
  - **entdecken** (discover; “uncover”)
  - **vergiften** (to poison)

** 5. Compound Words (Very Common in German)
*** a) Noun + Noun
- Hand + Schuh → **Handschuh** (glove)
- Wasser + Flasche → **Wasserflasche** (water bottle)

*** b) Adjective + Noun
- Hoch + Schule → **Hochschule** (university)

*** c) Verb Stem + Noun
- Lauf + Bahn → **Laufbahn** (career)

*** d) Rule of Compounds
- The **rightmost word** is the *main* meaning.

** 6. Recognition Patterns (Useful While Reading)
*** a) Gender Cues
- Usually **feminine**: *-ung*, *-heit*, *-keit*, *-schaft*  
- Always **neuter**: *-chen*, *-lein*

*** b) Reading Tip
- Strip off prefixes to find the **root**.
- Look at the **suffix** to guess meaning and gender.
